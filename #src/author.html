Online shopping makes up one-fifth of the retail economy. E-Commerce businesses have completely changed the concept of shopping. The traditional 'brick-and-mortar' model, which implies face-to-face sales, is having difficulties competing with web-based shops and platforms like Amazon and AliExpress. The lack of comfort and long lines in stores are just two of the many reasons that shoppers today are turning to online retailers.

Online businesses stand out because of their high flexibility and low operating costs. Moreover, online shopping trends have changed people's perception of shopping. Online shops often offer free delivery and returns, as well as save you money and time by avoiding a trip to the store.

Sorry to break it to you, but if your retail business still isn't online, you are most likely going to lose your existing customers and not gain new ones unless you launch a website. Imagine that you decide to move your retail business online; the first thing that you should understand is that the digital transformation takes more than a day or even a week. It should be wisely planned and implemented so as not to upset the business workflow, continuity, or quality.

The milestones of digital transformation include a setup that involves the initial configuration of backup systems, network, application development, testing, monitoring and executing processes, and others that maintain the production cycle. These processes will also require some assistance to make everything work smoothly.

The task of a DevOps team is to minimize misunderstandings between Development and Operations departments and to enable the Development team to test and deploy the new versions of applications in a straight-forward manner. Hence, DevOps for the retail industry is a solution that brings you one step closer to being a strong competitor.
